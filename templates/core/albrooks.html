üéØ Step 1: Determine "Always In" Direction

The market is ALWAYS either "Always In Long" or "Always In Short" - this tells you which side to trade from.

üìà ALWAYS IN LONG

  • Price above 20 EMA
  • Making higher highs and higher lows
  • Strong bull bars closing near highs
  • Pullbacks hold above prior lows
  • Buy signals work, sell signals fail
  • ONLY LOOK FOR LONGS

üìâ ALWAYS IN SHORT

  • Price below 20 EMA
  • Making lower highs and lower lows
  • Strong bear bars closing near lows
  • Rallies fail at prior highs
  • Sell signals work, buy signals fail
  • ONLY LOOK FOR SHORTS

‚ö° Critical Rule: NEVER Fade the Always In Direction

If market is Always In Long, don't take shorts (even if "looks overbought"). If Always In Short, don't take longs (even if "looks oversold"). This is the #1 cause of losses.

üìç Step 2: Identify Market Context

Context Characteristics What To Do
Strong Trend Large bars, small pullbacks, mostly closes near extremes Trade WITH trend, enter on pullbacks, let runners run
Weak Trend / Broad Channel Two-sided, deeper pullbacks, mix of bull/bear bars Trade both WITH trend (higher probability) and counter-trend scalps
Trading Range Repeated failures at top/bottom, mix of bar types Fade extremes, scalp both directions, tight stops
Breakout Large bars, gap, strong momentum, closes far from EMA Wait for pullback or 2nd entry, don't chase
Climax Parabolic move, extremely large bars, exhaustion Wait for range to form, don't try to pick tops/bottoms

üéØ Step 3: High Probability Setups for MNQ/MES

Two-Legged Pullback

HIGH PROBABILITY

Setup: In trend, price pulls back in two legs to 20 EMA or prior breakout point

Entry: 1 tick above high of signal bar (bull) or 1 tick below low (bear)

Stop: Below pullback low (bull) or above high (bear)

Target: 2x risk minimum, measured move, or swing target

Failed Breakout (Trap)

HIGH PROBABILITY

Setup: Breakout of range/swing fails within 5 bars, reverses hard

Entry: Enter on strong reversal bar in opposite direction

Stop: Beyond failed breakout extreme

Target: Opposite side of range or measured move

High/Low 2 (H2/L2)

HIGH PROBABILITY

Setup: Second attempt at prior high/low in trend

Entry: Pullback to EMA after first attempt fails, enter on 2nd signal

Stop: Below EMA or prior swing

Target: New swing high/low or measured move

Wedge Reversal

MEDIUM PROBABILITY

Setup: 3 pushes in same direction (wedge), final push shows weakness

Entry: After final push fails, enter reversal on strong bar

Stop: Beyond wedge extreme

Target: Start of wedge or measured move

Opening Reversal

MEDIUM PROBABILITY

Setup: Strong move in first 30 min, reverses at 10:00-10:30 ET

Entry: After momentum shift on strong reversal bar

Stop: Beyond opening extreme

Target: VWAP or opening price

Spike & Channel

HIGH PROBABILITY

Setup: Strong spike, then tight channel in same direction

Entry: Buy pullbacks to bottom of channel (if bull)

Stop: Below channel or below EMA

Target: Channel top, measured move, or let it run

üìä Key Price Action Signals

Strong Bull Bar

Large body, closes near high, little/no tail at top. Momentum.

Strong Bear Bar

Large body, closes near low, little/no tail at bottom. Momentum.

Doji / Inside Bar

Small body, indecision. Often precedes breakout or reversal.

Bull Reversal Bar

Large tail below, closes near high after selloff. Rejection of lows.

Bear Reversal Bar

Large tail above, closes near low after rally. Rejection of highs.

Outside Bar

Engulfs prior bar. Direction of close matters - strong signal if with trend.

Consecutive Bars

3+ bull/bear bars in row = strong momentum. Don't fade it.

Two Bar Reversal

Large bar followed by opposite bar that engulfs or exceeds it.

Breakout Bar

Closes far from prior range/swing. Wait for pullback to enter.

üîÑ Real-Time Decision Process (Every 5 Minutes)

STEP 1: What is Always In Direction? Price above 20 EMA + higher lows = Always In Long (only look for longs)
Price below 20 EMA + lower highs = Always In Short (only look for shorts)
Crossing EMA back and forth = Range (trade both ways, scalp mode)
STEP 2: Is this a strong trend or weak trend/range? Strong = large bars, small pullbacks, consecutive closes (trade WITH only)
Weak = two-sided, deeper pullbacks (can trade both directions)
Range = repeated failures at levels (fade extremes)
STEP 3: Where is price relative to structure? At prior high/low? ‚Üí Likely reversal or consolidation
At EMA after pullback? ‚Üí Likely continuation entry
Far from EMA after big move? ‚Üí Wait for pullback, don't chase
STEP 4: What is the last signal bar telling me? Strong bar WITH trend + closes near extreme = momentum continuing
Weak bar / doji after move = exhaustion, wait
Strong reversal bar AGAINST trend = possible reversal IF at key level
STEP 5: Is there a setup? Two-legged pullback complete? ‚Üí Enter
Failed breakout? ‚Üí Enter opposite direction
High/Low 2 setup? ‚Üí Enter on signal bar
Nothing clear? ‚Üí DO NOTHING (hardest rule to follow!)

‚úÖ Entry, Stop, and Exit Rules

Entry Methods

1. Stop Order (Preferred for MNQ/MES):
  • Long: 1 tick above signal bar high
  • Short: 1 tick below signal bar low
  • Ensures breakout of signal bar (confirmation)
2. Limit Order (Aggressive):
  • Enter during formation of signal bar
  • Risky - bar could fail
  • Only in very strong trends

Position Sizing

Al Brooks Method:
  • Risk 1-2% of account per trade
  • MNQ: $5/point, adjust contracts to risk
  • MES: $2.50/point, adjust contracts to risk
  • Always know your dollar risk BEFORE entering

üõë Stop Loss Placement

  • Trending Market: 1 tick below pullback low (long) or 1 tick above pullback high (short)
  • Range Market: 1 tick beyond opposite extreme of range
  • Signal Bar Stop: 1 tick beyond signal bar extreme (tighter but riskier)
  • Max Risk: For MNQ max 20 points, MES max 10 points (adjust for volatility)
  • Never move stop against you!

üéØ Profit Targets & Exits

  • Scalp (50% position): Take at 1:1 or 1.5:1 risk/reward
  • Runner (50% position): Trail stop or target measured move
  • Measured Move: Height of breakout pattern projected from breakout point
  • Swing Target: Prior high/low or significant resistance/support
  • Strong Trend: Trail below bars, exit on first reversal bar
  • Range: Exit at opposite extreme or if range breaks against you
  • Time Stop: Exit before major news or if trade not moving after 30-60 min

‚ö° MNQ/MES Specific Adjustments

MNQ (Micro Nasdaq)

Characteristics:

  • More volatile than MES
  • Wider stops needed (15-20 points typical)
  • Bigger point targets (20-40+ points in trends)
  • Best times: 9:30-11:30 AM, 2-4 PM ET
  • Watch tech sector news

Typical Targets:

  • Swing: 25-50 points
  • Runner: 50+ points in strong trends

Characteristics:

  • More stable than MNQ
  • Tighter stops work (8-12 points typical)
  • Smaller point targets (10-25 points)
  • Follows institutional flow better
  • Good for beginners

Typical Targets:

  • Scalp: 5-8 points
  • Swing: 12-25 points
  • Runner: 25+ points in strong trends

üö´ Common Mistakes (Al Brooks Warnings)

Mistakes That Kill Accounts:

  1. Fading strong trends - "It's too high to buy" is NOT a reason to short in Always In Long
  2. Taking every setup - Wait for high probability (context + setup + good signal bar)
  3. Revenge trading - After loss, traders force trades. Take a break instead.
  4. Moving stops against you - If wrong, get out. Don't give market more room.
  5. Not taking profits - Scale out. Don't be greedy. Protect winners.
  6. Trading chop - If market is unclear, DO NOTHING. Most important skill.
  7. Ignoring context - Same pattern works differently in trend vs range
  8. Tight stops in ranges - Ranges are choppy. Give more room or don't trade.
  9. Wide stops in trends - Trends allow tight stops at structure. Use them.
  10. Trading against institutional bias - If market wants to go up, it will. Don't fight it.
