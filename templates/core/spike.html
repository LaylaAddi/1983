üéØ What You've Discovered

You're describing what Al Brooks calls a "buying climax" or "exhaustion spike" - when price extends too far, too fast from the trend channel.

‚úÖ This IS a Recognized Strategy

Names for this setup:

  • Climax Scalp - Al Brooks terminology
  • Exhaustion Fade - Mean reversion play
  • Blow-off Top Scalp (in uptrend) or Selling Climax Scalp (in downtrend)
  • Channel Overshoot - Price extends beyond channel, snaps back

Why it works: Price moved too far, too fast. Needs to "catch its breath" and pull back to the moving average or channel. You're not predicting a reversal - just a temporary pause/retracement.

üìà Visual Example

What the chart shows: Price is in an uptrend (above EMA), makes a parabolic spike outside the channel with large bars. This creates a buying climax. You short the exhaustion for a quick scalp back to the channel/EMA.

‚úÖ When This Works (High Probability)

‚úÖ TRADE IT When:

  • Weak/Broad Channel Trend - Not a raging bull run
  • Multiple large bars in a row - 3-5+ big bull bars
  • Price far from EMA - 20+ points on MNQ, 10+ on MES
  • Parabolic move - Accelerating, not steady
  • Large tails appear - Bulls getting rejected
  • Volume spike - Often climaxes on volume
  • Two-sided bars - Big wicks = exhaustion
  • After 2+ hours of trending - Trend is tired
  • Near round numbers - MNQ 21500, 22000, etc.
  • Midday (11 AM-1 PM) - Lower energy time

üö´ AVOID When:

  • Strong trend channel - Tight, consecutive bars
  • Breaking major resistance - Likely to continue
  • Opening 30 min (9:30-10 AM) - Too much momentum
  • After Fed/economic news - Trends can run for hours
  • Gap and go pattern - Morning gap up continuing
  • First spike of the day - Often has follow-through
  • Very strong Always In direction - Don't fade power
  • Low volume spike - Fake exhaustion
  • Small bars - Not a real climax
  • Fresh trend - Trend just started, has legs

üìã Entry Rules (Critical!)

Signal Bar Requirements:

  • Large bar with tail at top - Bears rejecting the high
  • Doji or inside bar - Momentum stalling
  • Strong reversal bar - Opens high, closes near low with big range
  • Two-bar reversal - Big bull bar followed by big bear bar that engulfs it

‚ö†Ô∏è Critical Rules (MUST Follow!)

üö® This is SCALPING Only - Not Swing Trading!

  1. TIGHT STOPS - If spike continues, you're wrong. Get out immediately. Don't hope.
  2. QUICK EXITS - Exit at EMA or channel top. Don't get greedy. Take 5-15 points on MNQ, 3-8 on MES.
  3. TIME LIMIT - If trade doesn't move within 5-10 min, exit at breakeven. This is quick or nothing.
  4. ONLY 1 CONTRACT - Don't size up on counter-trend trades. Risk is higher.
  5. RESPECT THE TREND - You're borrowing against the trend. Trend can resume anytime.
  6. MAX 1-2 PER DAY - These are low-frequency, opportunistic scalps. Don't force them.
  7. IF WRONG, FLIP - If stopped out and trend continues, consider going WITH trend instead.
  8. NEVER add to losers - Don't average into a counter-trend position. Recipe for disaster.

üí° Al Brooks on Counter-Trend Scalping

Key Quotes and Concepts:

"In a strong trend, every reversal attempt fails. But after a climax, even a strong trend needs to pause."

"The problem with counter-trend trading is you can be right 8 times and wrong once, and that one time wipes out everything."

"Counter-trend scalps work best in trading ranges and broad channels. In strong trends, don't fight the tape."

When Al Brooks Says to Take It:

  • After 3+ consecutive large bars in one direction
  • Price is far from EMA (2-3 times average distance)
  • Late in the trend (after 1-2 hours of trending)
  • Reversal bar is strong (large range, closes near opposite extreme)
  • You're in a broad bull channel, not a tight trend channel

When Al Brooks Says to AVOID:

  • During strong trend channels with consecutive bars
  • First spike of the day - often has follow-through
  • After major news - momentum can last hours
  • When breakout just happened - let it develop first
  • If you can't define a logical stop

üéì Summary: The Trade-Off

‚úÖ Pros:

  • High reward:risk (often 2:1 or better)
  • Quick trade (5-15 min)
  • Obvious stop placement
  • 60-70% win rate when done right
  • Clear target (EMA/channel)
  • Low time commitment

‚ùå Cons:

  • Counter-trend = higher risk
  • Can get stopped out quickly
  • Requires discipline to exit fast
  • Easy to overtrade (forcing it)
  • One big loser can wipe out many wins
  • Requires experience to read exhaustion

üéØ Bottom Line:

This is a valid strategy that works - BUT it's advanced. New traders should focus on WITH-trend trades first. Once you're consistently profitable trading WITH the trend, THEN add these counter-trend scalps as occasional opportunities. Think of it as a bonus trade, not your bread and butter.
